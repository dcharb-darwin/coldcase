## §13663(a) — Disclosure on the report

Law enforcement agencies must maintain a policy requiring that an official report prepared using artificial intelligence (fully or partially) include:

**(a)(1)** Identification of the AI program(s) used, **plus this exact statement**:

> "This report was written either fully or in part using artificial intelligence."

**(a)(2)** The **signature** of the law enforcement officer or member of the agency who prepared the report, in physical or electronic form, **verifying that they reviewed the contents of that report and that the facts contained in the official report are true and correct.**

## §13663(b) — First-draft retention

If AI is used (fully or partially) to create an official report, **the first draft created shall be retained by the agency for as long as the official report is retained.**

Drafts (other than the final report) **shall not constitute an officer's statement.**

## §13663(c) — Audit trail

The agency must maintain an audit trail identifying:

**(c)(1)** The person who used AI.
**(c)(2)** The video and audio footage used to create the report, if any.

## §13663(d) — Vendor restrictions

A vendor that contracts with a law enforcement agency to provide AI services for report generation **shall not share, sell, or otherwise use the information provided by the agency**, except:

- to provide the contracted service to that agency,
- to comply with a court order, or
- for troubleshooting, bias mitigation, accuracy improvement, or system refinement.

## §13663(e) — Definitions

- **"Artificial intelligence"** — a system that "infers from the input it receives how to generate outputs," covering both narrative-drafting from camera footage and generative-AI report enhancement.
- **"Official report"** — the **final version** of the report that is signed by the officer.
- **"First draft"** — the initial document or narrative produced **solely by artificial intelligence.**
