# Model Contract Clause — AI Vendor Data Handling Under California Penal Code §13663(d)

**Document type:** Model contract clause for incorporation by reference into a master services agreement (or as an addendum) between a California law enforcement agency and an AI vendor providing report-generation services.
**Statutory anchor:** California Penal Code §13663(d), effective 2026-01-01.
**Runtime anchor:** Cold Case `VendorAccessRequest` workflow and `services/vendor_scope.py` enforcement gate.
**Audience:** City attorney, agency procurement, vendor counsel.
**Status:** Template — fields in [BRACKETS] are negotiable. Have the city attorney review before execution.

> **How to use this template.** Replace bracketed placeholders. Confirm Appendix A (subprocessor list) is current as of execution. Attach as Exhibit [X] to the master services agreement. The Order of Precedence section (§13) is load-bearing — do not delete it.

## Clause [X]. AI Vendor Data Handling and Use Restrictions (Penal Code §13663(d))

### 1. Recitals

1.1 **Agency status.** [AGENCY NAME] (the "Agency") is a law enforcement agency within the meaning of California Penal Code §13663 and is subject to the obligations imposed by that statute as of its effective date, January 1, 2026. The Agency's officers prepare official reports, in whole or in part, with the assistance of artificial intelligence services.

1.2 **Vendor role.** [VENDOR NAME] ("Vendor") provides artificial intelligence services used, in whole or in part, by Agency personnel to prepare official reports within the meaning of Penal Code §13663(e), and in that capacity processes information provided by the Agency to the Vendor's systems.

1.3 **Statutory restriction.** Penal Code §13663(d) restricts Vendor's ability to share, sell, or otherwise use information provided by the Agency, and permits only the enumerated purposes set forth in that subsection. The Agency cannot satisfy its obligations under §13663(d) without contractual undertakings from Vendor that mirror, at minimum, the statutory restriction and its carve-outs.

1.4 **Material condition.** The Agency requires the inclusion of this Clause as a material condition of any contract under which Vendor processes Agency Data, and the parties agree that Vendor's compliance with this Clause is essential to the bargain. A material breach of this Clause is a material breach of the master agreement.

1.5 **Software alignment.** The parties further acknowledge that the Agency's deployment of Vendor's AI Services is governed at runtime by the Cold Case platform's `VendorAccessRequest` workflow, and that this Clause is drafted to align the contractual carve-outs in §13663(d) with the software gates enforced by that workflow, so that the contract and the system use the same vocabulary. Where this Clause refers to a `VendorAccessPurpose` enum value, the parties intend that the contractual term and the software identifier have the same meaning.

1.6 **No waiver of public-records rights.** Nothing in this Clause limits, modifies, or waives any obligation of the Agency under the California Public Records Act, the California Constitution, or any applicable discovery rule, and nothing limits the Agency's right to disclose information in its possession (including Agency Data returned to the Agency) as required by those authorities.

### 2. Definitions

For purposes of this Clause:

2.1 **"Agency Data"** means any data, content, prompt, response, document, image, audio, video, metadata, or derived work product that is (a) provided by the Agency or Agency personnel to the AI Services, (b) generated by the AI Services in response to Agency input, or (c) derived in whole or in part from items in (a) or (b). Agency Data includes, without limitation, the "first draft" of any official report within the meaning of Penal Code §13663(e), all prompts and responses retained in any conversation log, and any case-, report-, or person-identifying metadata.

2.2 **"AI Services"** means the artificial intelligence service(s) provided by Vendor under the master agreement, including any system that "infers from the input it receives how to generate outputs" within the meaning of Penal Code §13663(e).

2.3 **"Official Report"** has the meaning given in Penal Code §13663(e) — the final version of the report that is signed by the officer.

2.4 **"First Draft"** has the meaning given in Penal Code §13663(e) — the initial document or narrative produced solely by artificial intelligence.

2.5 **"Permitted Purpose"** means one of the six purposes enumerated in §3 of this Clause, and no other purpose.

2.6 **"Court Order"** means a subpoena, search warrant, court order, or other compulsory legal process issued by a court of competent jurisdiction and directed to Vendor or its subprocessor in respect of Agency Data.

2.7 **"Authorized Personnel"** means individual Vendor employees, contractors, or subprocessor personnel who (a) have a documented need to access Agency Data for a Permitted Purpose, (b) are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Clause, and (c) have an open and currently-valid `VendorAccessRequest` in the Cold Case platform.

2.8 **"VendorAccessRequest"** means the workflow implemented in the Cold Case platform whereby a member of Vendor's personnel declares purpose, scope, and time window, obtains Agency approval, and creates an immutable audit record of each subsequent access.

2.9 **"Subprocessor"** means any third party engaged by Vendor to process Agency Data on Vendor's behalf, including hosting providers, foundation-model providers, and observability vendors.

### 3. Permitted Purposes (the §13663(d) Carve-Outs)

3.1 **Statutory text.** Vendor acknowledges that §13663(d) provides, in relevant part:

> "A vendor that contracts with a law enforcement agency to provide artificial intelligence services for the generation of an official report shall not share, sell, or otherwise use the information provided by the agency to the vendor, except: (i) to provide the contracted service to that agency; (ii) to comply with a court order; or (iii) for troubleshooting, bias mitigation, accuracy improvement, or system refinement."

3.2 **Closed list.** Vendor shall use Agency Data only for the following six Permitted Purposes, which together constitute the complete and exclusive set of uses authorized under this Clause. Each Permitted Purpose is paired with the `VendorAccessPurpose` enum value used by the Cold Case software gate; the contract vocabulary and the software vocabulary are intended to mean the same thing.

#### 3.2.1 Providing the Contracted AI Service to the Agency

- **Statutory basis:** §13663(d)(i) — "to provide the contracted service to that agency."
- **Cold Case enum value:** No `VendorAccessRequest` is required for this purpose, because routine service delivery operates inside the tenant-isolated production path under the Agency's own user identities, not Vendor personnel identities. Vendor personnel do not directly read Agency Data during ordinary service delivery.
- **In scope:** Serving real-time inference, processing prompts and returning responses, storing the resulting first-draft text in the Agency tenant per the master agreement, and operating the infrastructure required to do so.
- **Not in scope:** Any access by Vendor personnel to the content of Agency prompts or responses for any reason other than (a) automated processing required to return a response or (b) one of the other Permitted Purposes below; copying Agency Data out of the Agency tenant for any reason other than a Permitted Purpose; deriving aggregate analytics across customers that reveal Agency-specific facts.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** Tenant isolation is enforced at the data layer by `tenant_id` filtering on every read; Vendor personnel logging in as themselves (not as an Agency user) and lacking an active `VendorAccessRequest` cannot read Agency Data at all, because the application enforces a `requesting_operator_id`-based gate in `services/vendor_scope.py:resolve_active_scope`.

#### 3.2.2 Compliance with a Court Order

- **Statutory basis:** §13663(d)(ii) — "to comply with a court order."
- **Cold Case enum value:** Court-order compliance is handled by the workflow in §9 of this Clause and is not a routine `VendorAccessPurpose` enum value at this time. Any production necessitated by a Court Order shall, in addition to the §9 process, be recorded via a `VendorAccessRequest` with `reason_detail` containing the Court Order identifier so the access is captured in the immutable audit log.
- **In scope:** Producing the minimum Agency Data required to comply with a facially valid Court Order, after the notice and tailoring obligations in §9.
- **Not in scope:** Voluntary production in response to informal law-enforcement inquiry; production in response to a subpoena that the Agency has timely moved to quash; production beyond the minimum scope of the Court Order.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** Even a Court-Order-driven access is logged: each `VendorAccessRequest.accessed_at` entry is append-only, and the surrounding `AuditEvent` chain is hash-linked, so the production is not deniable after the fact.

#### 3.2.3 Troubleshooting

- **Statutory basis:** §13663(d)(iii) — "troubleshooting."
- **Cold Case enum value:** `VendorAccessPurpose.TROUBLESHOOTING` (`"troubleshooting"`).
- **In scope:** Investigation of a specific incident, defect, outage, or anomalous behavior reported by the Agency, identified by Vendor's monitoring, or surfaced by the Cold Case Refusal & Anomaly Report. The `reason_detail` field of the `VendorAccessRequest` shall reference the ticket, incident, or anomaly identifier giving rise to the investigation.
- **Not in scope:** Open-ended product analytics; "fishing" through Agency Data without a defect hypothesis; usage-pattern studies; reconstruction of investigations for case studies or marketing materials.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** A troubleshooting request creates a `VendorAccessRequest` with `purpose="troubleshooting"`, an Agency-approved scope (`tenant_wide`, `case_ids`, or `report_ids`), and an `expires_at` time window. Every off-scope read returns HTTP 403 and emits a `VENDOR_ACCESS_SCOPE_VIOLATION` audit event via `services/vendor_scope.py:enforce_vendor_scope`.

#### 3.2.4 Bias Mitigation

- **Statutory basis:** §13663(d)(iii) — "bias mitigation."
- **Cold Case enum value:** `VendorAccessPurpose.BIAS_MITIGATION` (`"bias_mitigation"`).
- **In scope:** Targeted evaluation of model behavior for disparate impact on protected classes, demographic-error analysis tied to documented mitigation work, and evaluation-set construction in which Agency Data is reviewed by Authorized Personnel solely to identify and reduce bias in model outputs.
- **Not in scope:** Use of Agency Data to train foundation models without the separate written authorization required by §4.3; transfer of Agency Data to third-party bias-evaluation services not named as Subprocessors in Appendix A; publication of Agency case facts in academic or marketing materials, even with redaction.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** Bias-mitigation requests appear in the Anomaly Report (F17) for routine review by the city auditor, alongside every other `vendor.access.used` event.

#### 3.2.5 Accuracy Improvement

- **Statutory basis:** §13663(d)(iii) — "accuracy improvement."
- **Cold Case enum value:** `VendorAccessPurpose.ACCURACY_IMPROVEMENT` (`"accuracy_improvement"`).
- **In scope:** Review of model outputs against ground truth supplied by the Agency, error categorization, regression evaluation, and the construction of internal evaluation sets used to measure accuracy improvements release-over-release. Such evaluation sets shall remain inside the Agency tenant unless the Agency has provided the separate written authorization described in §4.3.
- **Not in scope:** Cross-customer accuracy benchmarking that exposes Agency-specific facts; sale or sharing of evaluation sets; use of Agency Data to demonstrate accuracy improvements to other prospects or customers.

#### 3.2.6 System Refinement

- **Statutory basis:** §13663(d)(iii) — "system refinement."
- **Cold Case enum value:** `VendorAccessPurpose.SYSTEM_REFINEMENT` (`"system_refinement"`).
- **In scope:** Engineering changes to the AI Services informed by observation of Agency Data — for example, prompt-template revision, retrieval-strategy adjustment, guardrail tuning, latency improvements, and reliability work — where the observation is bounded by an approved `VendorAccessRequest`.
- **Not in scope:** New-feature ideation untethered to a specific Agency-reported need; transfer of refinement learnings into materials shared with other customers in a way that exposes Agency-specific facts; product-management research that does not produce a corresponding engineering change.

3.3 **No purpose creep.** Any use of Agency Data that does not fall squarely within one of §3.2.1–§3.2.6 is prohibited. Doubt as to whether a given use is within scope shall be resolved against use, and the Vendor shall consult the Agency in writing before proceeding.

3.4 **Minimum-necessary principle.** Even within a Permitted Purpose, Vendor shall access only the Agency Data reasonably necessary to accomplish that purpose. Where a narrower scope (`case_ids` or `report_ids`) is reasonably sufficient, Vendor shall not seek `tenant_wide` access, and the Agency may deny `tenant_wide` requests that do not articulate a specific need for breadth.

3.5 **Documentation.** For each Permitted Purpose other than §3.2.1, Vendor shall record in the `reason_detail` field of the corresponding `VendorAccessRequest` a free-text justification sufficient for an auditor reviewing the record one year later to understand why the access was needed and what was done with it. Generic justifications ("debugging", "evaluation") are insufficient.

### 4. Prohibited Uses

4.1 **No sale.** Vendor shall not sell, license, rent, or otherwise transfer Agency Data, or any derivative work or analytic product based on Agency Data, to any third party, for any consideration, monetary or otherwise.

4.2 **No third-party sharing.** Vendor shall not share Agency Data with any third party other than (a) Subprocessors listed in Appendix A and bound by §6 of this Clause, (b) recipients of Court-Order productions under §9, or (c) parties to whom the Agency directs Vendor in writing to disclose.

4.3 **No foundation-model training.** Vendor shall not use Agency Data, directly or as part of a training corpus, to train, fine-tune, distill, or otherwise improve any foundation model, embedding model, classifier, or other machine-learning artifact that is or may be used outside the Agency tenant, except under a separate written authorization signed by the Agency that specifies the model, the dataset scope, the retention period, and the disposition of the resulting weights. Aggregate metrics (e.g., counts of refusals, latency percentiles) that do not embed Agency-identifying facts are not "Agency Data" for purposes of this §4.3.

4.4 **No marketing or research.** Vendor shall not use Agency Data for marketing, sales enablement, case studies, white papers, conference presentations, blog posts, demonstrations to prospects, or external research publication, in identifiable, redacted, or aggregated form, without the Agency's prior written consent.

4.5 **No cross-customer benchmarking.** Vendor shall not include Agency Data in any cross-customer benchmark, leaderboard, or competitive analysis that exposes Agency-specific facts to any third party.

4.6 **No re-identification.** If Vendor at any time generates a de-identified or anonymized derivative of Agency Data for an internal Permitted Purpose, Vendor shall not attempt to re-identify the underlying individuals or cases, and shall not share that derivative externally except as permitted by this Clause.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** Cold Case stores prompts, responses, and the first-draft snapshot inside the Agency tenant; it does not transmit Agency Data to any training pipeline. The model identifiers captured in `Report.ai_programs_used[]` are the provider-returned ids that processed the inference at runtime, not configured aliases — so an after-the-fact change of model would be detectable in the audit trail.

### 5. Access Controls

5.1 **VendorAccessRequest required.** Vendor personnel shall access Agency Data only through the Cold Case `VendorAccessRequest` workflow. Each request shall declare:

  (a) **Purpose** — one of the `VendorAccessPurpose` enum values listed in §3.2 (`troubleshooting`, `bias_mitigation`, `accuracy_improvement`, `system_refinement`), or, for the carve-outs in §3.2.1 (contracted service) and §3.2.2 (court order), the corresponding workflow described in those sections;
  (b) **Scope** — `tenant_wide`, a specific list of `case_ids`, or a specific list of `report_ids`. Scope shall be the narrowest reasonably sufficient for the Permitted Purpose;
  (c) **Time window** — a defined `expires_at` after which the request shall not authorize further access. Time windows shall be no longer than [72 HOURS] absent a documented need recorded in `reason_detail`.

5.2 **Agency approval.** No `VendorAccessRequest` shall confer access until approved by an Agency administrator. Denials, including the denial reason, are logged. The Agency may revoke an approved request at any time.

5.3 **Immutable logging.** Every act of access pursuant to an approved `VendorAccessRequest` shall append an entry to `accessed_at` and increment `use_count`. These records, together with the surrounding hash-linked `AuditEvent` chain, are tamper-evident and shall not be modified or deleted by Vendor.

5.4 **Off-scope blocking.** Vendor agrees that the Cold Case platform shall return HTTP 403 and emit a `VENDOR_ACCESS_SCOPE_VIOLATION` audit event for any access attempt that falls outside the declared scope of the active `VendorAccessRequest`, and that such blocking is a material protection of the bargain — not a defect — and shall not give rise to any claim against the Agency.

5.5 **Authorized Personnel only.** Vendor shall ensure that only Authorized Personnel hold credentials capable of opening a `VendorAccessRequest`, and shall promptly disable such credentials when an individual ceases to be Authorized Personnel.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** The runtime gate lives in `services/vendor_scope.py`. `resolve_active_scope` returns the most recently-approved unexpired `VendorAccessRequest` for the operator+tenant; `_is_in_scope` resolves `tenant_wide` / `case_ids` / `report_ids` semantics; `enforce_vendor_scope` 403s and audits off-scope reads. Expiry is enforced by `auto_expire_if_due`, which flips `APPROVED` → `EXPIRED` on inspection — an expired request is never silently treated as active.

### 6. Subprocessors

6.1 **Disclosed list.** Vendor shall maintain a current list of Subprocessors that process Agency Data, including the Subprocessor's name, the function performed, and the country of processing. The list as of the Effective Date is attached as **Appendix A**. Vendor shall make the current list available to the Agency on request and shall provide [30 DAYS] advance notice of any addition or replacement.

6.2 **Flow-down terms.** Vendor shall not engage any Subprocessor to process Agency Data unless that Subprocessor is bound, in writing, to obligations no less protective than those in this Clause, including without limitation §3 (Permitted Purposes), §4 (Prohibited Uses), §5 (Access Controls), §7 (Incident Response), and §9 (Court Orders).

6.3 **Liability for Subprocessors.** Vendor remains fully liable to the Agency for any act or omission of a Subprocessor that would, if performed by Vendor, constitute a breach of this Clause.

6.4 **Right to object.** The Agency may object in writing to a proposed Subprocessor within [15 DAYS] of notice. If the parties cannot resolve the objection within [30 DAYS], the Agency may terminate the affected AI Services without penalty and exercise its rights under §10 (Termination and Data Return).

### 7. Incident Response

7.1 **Notice.** Vendor shall notify the Agency without undue delay and in any event within [24 HOURS] of becoming aware of: (a) any unauthorized access to, acquisition of, loss of, or disclosure of Agency Data; (b) any suspected breach of this Clause by Vendor or any Subprocessor; (c) any malicious code, intrusion, or exfiltration affecting systems that process Agency Data; or (d) any receipt of a Court Order that touches Agency Data (subject to §9.1 below).

7.2 **Content of notice.** The notice shall include, to the extent then known, (a) the nature and timeline of the event, (b) the Agency Data implicated, (c) the Vendor personnel investigating, (d) remedial steps taken or planned, and (e) any obligations the Agency may have to notify third parties, regulators, or data subjects.

7.3 **Cooperation.** Vendor shall cooperate fully with the Agency's investigation, shall preserve all logs and forensic artifacts (including the immutable `VendorAccessRequest` audit records), and shall not communicate publicly about the event without Agency consent except as required by law.

7.4 **Remediation cost.** Where the event arises from Vendor's breach of this Clause, Vendor shall bear the reasonable cost of investigation, notification, and remediation, subject to §12 (Indemnity).

### 8. Audit Rights

8.1 **Right to audit.** The Agency, or its designee (including without limitation the [AGENCY CITY ATTORNEY] or an independent auditor retained by the Agency), may audit Vendor's compliance with this Clause on [30 DAYS] reasonable written notice, during normal business hours, and in a manner reasonably designed to minimize disruption to Vendor's operations.

8.2 **Frequency.** Audits shall be conducted no more than [ONCE PER CALENDAR YEAR], except that the Agency may conduct additional audits without limit following any event described in §7.1 or any reasonable suspicion of breach of this Clause.

8.3 **Scope.** The audit may cover (a) `VendorAccessRequest` records and their corresponding `AuditEvent` chains, (b) Subprocessor flow-down documentation, (c) policies and training materials governing Authorized Personnel, (d) incident-response records, and (e) any other reasonable subject matter directly relevant to Vendor's obligations under this Clause.

8.4 **Reliance on Cold Case audit artifacts.** The parties acknowledge that the Cold Case platform produces audit artifacts (F4 audit export, F7 chain-of-custody PDF, F16 AI Program Inventory, F17 Refusal & Anomaly Report) that are admissible audit evidence under this §8. The Agency may rely on those artifacts in lieu of additional discovery for facts they cover.

8.5 **Findings.** Vendor shall remediate any material non-compliance identified by the audit within [30 DAYS] or such other period as the parties agree in writing. Repeated or willful non-compliance is a material breach.

> **Cold Case enforcement note.** The hash-linked `AuditEvent` chain (each event references `prev_event_hash`) makes tampering with the audit trail detectable; an auditor recomputing the chain can confirm completeness without relying on Vendor's good faith.

### 9. Court Orders

9.1 **Notice to Agency.** Vendor shall notify the Agency in writing of any Court Order it receives that touches Agency Data, before producing any Agency Data in response, unless legally prohibited from doing so. Where notice is prohibited, Vendor shall notify the Agency as soon as the prohibition lapses.

9.2 **Minimization.** Vendor shall produce only the minimum Agency Data reasonably required by the Court Order, and shall, where feasible, redact or narrow the scope before production. Vendor shall cooperate with any motion to quash, narrow, or modify the Court Order brought by the Agency, at the Agency's expense.

9.3 **Logging.** Each production made pursuant to a Court Order shall be (a) recorded in a `VendorAccessRequest` whose `reason_detail` references the Court Order identifier, (b) logged via the corresponding `AuditEvent` chain, and (c) confirmed to the Agency in writing within [5 BUSINESS DAYS] of production, identifying the data produced, the recipient, and the legal basis.

9.4 **No voluntary production.** Vendor shall not voluntarily produce Agency Data in response to any informal request, civil discovery in matters to which the Agency is not a party, or any process that does not constitute a Court Order, without the Agency's prior written authorization.

### 10. Termination and Data Return

10.1 **Return or destruction.** On termination or expiration of the master agreement, or on the Agency's earlier written direction, Vendor shall, at the Agency's election, return all Agency Data to the Agency in a machine-readable format reasonably designated by the Agency, or destroy all copies of Agency Data using methods consistent with NIST SP 800-88 or successor guidance, in either case within [30 DAYS].

10.2 **Subprocessor obligations.** Vendor shall ensure that each Subprocessor performs the same return-or-destroy obligation within the same period.

10.3 **Attestation.** Vendor shall provide the Agency with a written attestation, signed by an officer of Vendor, certifying that all Agency Data has been returned or destroyed as required by this §10, identifying any Agency Data that Vendor is legally required to retain and the basis for such retention.

10.4 **Backups.** Backups containing Agency Data that cannot reasonably be destroyed without affecting other data may be retained until expiration of Vendor's normal backup-retention cycle, provided that (a) such backups remain subject to all obligations of this Clause, (b) no Permitted Purpose is performed against them other than restoration in a documented disaster-recovery event, and (c) they are destroyed at the end of the retention cycle.

### 11. Survival

11.1 The following provisions survive termination or expiration of the master agreement: §2 (Definitions), §3 (Permitted Purposes) and §4 (Prohibited Uses) as to any Agency Data retained pursuant to §10.4, §5.3 (Immutable Logging), §7 (Incident Response) for events arising from pre-termination conduct, §8 (Audit Rights) for [TWO YEARS] following termination, §9 (Court Orders) as to any retained Agency Data, §10 (Termination and Data Return), §11 (Survival), §12 (Indemnity), and §13 (Order of Precedence).

### 12. Indemnity

12.1 **Vendor indemnity.** Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Agency and its officers, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claim, action, demand, loss, liability, judgment, settlement, or expense (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to (a) Vendor's breach of this Clause, (b) any act or omission of a Subprocessor that would constitute a breach if performed by Vendor, or (c) any unauthorized access to, use of, or disclosure of Agency Data caused by Vendor or its Subprocessors.

12.2 **Conditions.** The Agency shall provide Vendor with prompt written notice of the claim, sole control of the defense and settlement (provided that no settlement shall impose any non-monetary obligation on the Agency without the Agency's written consent), and reasonable cooperation at Vendor's expense.

12.3 **No cap.** The parties agree that any limitation of liability in the master agreement shall not apply to Vendor's indemnification obligations under this §12 to the extent the underlying claim arises from Vendor's breach of §4 (Prohibited Uses) or §3 (Permitted Purposes).

### 13. Order of Precedence

13.1 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between this Clause and the master agreement, this Clause shall control with respect to AI Services governed by Penal Code §13663 and the handling of Agency Data thereunder. All other provisions of the master agreement shall remain in full force and effect.

13.2 No provision of the master agreement, and no amendment, course of dealing, or course of performance, shall be construed to waive, modify, or limit any obligation imposed on Vendor by Penal Code §13663 or by this Clause.

13.3 If any provision of this Clause is held invalid or unenforceable, the remainder shall be enforced to the maximum extent permitted by law, and the invalid provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving its intent.

## Implementation Notes (Non-Operative — for Drafter Reference Only)

The following notes are informational, not part of the executed Clause. They are retained here so that future maintainers of this template understand the alignment between the contract language and the Cold Case runtime.

1. **Enum alignment.** The four `VendorAccessPurpose` enum values defined in `server-py/models/vendor_access.py` are `TROUBLESHOOTING`, `BIAS_MITIGATION`, `ACCURACY_IMPROVEMENT`, and `SYSTEM_REFINEMENT`. The contract enumerates six Permitted Purposes; the two additional ones (contracted-service delivery and Court-Order compliance) are deliberately not represented as routine enum values because (a) contracted-service delivery does not entail Vendor-personnel reads of Agency Data and (b) Court-Order compliance is rare and is governed by the §9 workflow, with a `VendorAccessRequest` recorded after the fact for auditability. If the engineering team later adds `COURT_ORDER` or `CONTRACTED_SERVICE` enum values, update §3.2.1 and §3.2.2 of this template to reference them.

2. **Scope kinds.** The `VendorAccessScopeKind` values (`tenant_wide`, `case_ids`, `report_ids`) are referenced by name in §5.1(b). If those are renamed in code, update §5.1(b).

3. **Audit-event type.** The `VENDOR_ACCESS_SCOPE_VIOLATION` event type is referenced in §5.4 and §8.4. If renamed in `models/audit_event.py`, update those references.

4. **Time windows.** [24 HOURS] (§7.1) and [72 HOURS] (§5.1(c)) are starting points. Some agencies prefer shorter incident-notice windows (e.g., 12 hours for known unauthorized access) and may negotiate down. Vendor-side counsel typically pushes for 72 hours on §7.1; the city attorney should defend 24-48 absent compelling reason.

5. **Audit frequency.** [ONCE PER CALENDAR YEAR] (§8.2) is a defensible baseline; agencies with heightened oversight obligations (e.g., consent-decree monitoring) may require quarterly.

6. **Indemnity cap carve-out.** §12.3 carves indemnity for §3/§4 breaches out of any master-agreement liability cap. This is the single most-negotiated provision; the city attorney should not concede a cap for these breaches given that they implicate criminal-procedure compliance and the agency's standing in court.

7. **Survival period for audit rights.** [TWO YEARS] (§11.1) tracks typical record-retention statutes; conform to the agency's actual retention rule for AI-assisted reports if longer.

8. **Court-order notice.** §9.1's "before producing" language is intentionally strong. Vendor counsel will sometimes propose "as soon as practicable"; resist that softening unless paired with a fixed minimum notice period (e.g., 5 business days) and a Vendor obligation to seek a protective order in the interim.
